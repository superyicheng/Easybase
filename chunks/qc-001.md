Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) feasibility assessment for modeling enzyme active sites relevant to fungal wood decay.

Target: Laccase active site (Cu₂ center), ~20 electrons in active space.
Current hardware: 127-qubit IBM Eagle, ~300 CNOT gate depth before decoherence.

Required qubits: ~40 (minimal active space) to ~80 (with dynamic correlation).
Required circuit depth: ~2000 CNOT gates for chemical accuracy (1 kcal/mol).

Verdict: NOT feasible on current hardware. Gap is ~7× in circuit depth.

Timeline estimate:
- 2027: IBM Heron (5000+ qubits, improved error rates) — marginal feasibility
- 2028-2029: Error-corrected logical qubits — likely feasible

Alternative: Use classical CASSCF for the active site, embed in MM force field (QM/MM). This gives reaction barriers within ~3 kcal/mol, sufficient for growth model rate constants.

The growth model (cbt-001) currently uses empirical rate constants. Quantum-derived rates would improve accuracy for novel substrates where experimental data is unavailable.
