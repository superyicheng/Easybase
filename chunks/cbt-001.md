Hyphal tip extension follows a vesicle supply center (VSC) model. Growth rate depends on:
- Vesicle production rate at the Spitzenkörper
- Turgor pressure differential across the tip wall
- Wall plasticity at the apex

Key equation: dL/dt = k × P × φ(wall_thickness)

Where k is the extensibility coefficient (typically 0.3–0.8 μm/min/MPa for Postia placenta), P is turgor pressure (~0.5 MPa), and φ is the wall yield function.

Branching occurs when the VSC bifurcates, creating two new tips. Branch angle correlates with nutrient gradient steepness. In isotropic media, branching is stochastic with mean interval ~200 μm.

Growth is not diffusion-limited in early stages — the fungus extends faster than substrate depletes locally. Depletion zones form later as biomass accumulates.
